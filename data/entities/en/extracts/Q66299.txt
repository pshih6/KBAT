The poet Ava (c. 1060 – 7 February 1127), also known as Frau Ava, Ava of Göttweig or Ava of Melk, was the first named female writer in any genre in the German language. She is the author of five poems which focused on Christian themes of salvation and the second coming of Christ. She is known for her simple rhyming couplets written in the vernacular, making complex biblical teachings accessible to the people of her time.
