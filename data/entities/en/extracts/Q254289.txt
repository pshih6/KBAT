Libuše Šafránková (born 7 June 1953 in Brno, Czechoslovakia) is a Czech actress. Her husband is actor Josef Abrhám.
Her breakthrough was the title role in the 1973 film Three Nuts for Cinderella, which is considered a Christmas film classic in many parts of Europe. In the 1970s and 1980s, she played leading roles in many fairy-tale films. She received the Czech Lion Award as best actress in a film in 1996 and then in 2008 she received the prize Hvězda mého srdce (Star of My Heart), awarded by Czech Television.
