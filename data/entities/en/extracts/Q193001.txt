Sighișoara (Romanian pronunciation: [siɡiˈʃo̯ara]; German: Schäßburg pronounced [ˈʃɛsbʊɐ̯k]; Transylvanian Saxon: Schäsbrich; Hungarian: Segesvár, pronounced [ˈʃɛɡɛʃvaːr] (listen); Yiddish: שעסבורג‎, Shesburg; Latin: Castrum Sex) is a city on the Târnava Mare River in Mureș County, Romania. Located in the historic region of Transylvania, Sighișoara has a population of 28,102 according to the 2011 census. It is a popular tourist destination for its well-preserved walled old town, which is listed by UNESCO as a World Heritage Site.
The city administers seven villages: Angofa, Aurel Vlaicu, Hetiur, Rora, Șoromiclea, Venchi and Viilor.
