Michel de Ghelderode (3 April 1898 – 1 April 1962) was an avant-garde Belgian dramatist, of Flanders, who spoke and wrote in French. His works often deal with the extremes of human experience, from death and degradation to religious exaltation. His name, Michel de Ghelderode, is a pseudonym, he was born Adémar Adolphe Louis Martens. He wrote plays and short stories, and was a noted letter writer.
