José Horacio Gómez (born December 26, 1951) is a Mexican-born American prelate of the Catholic Church. He has been the fifth Archbishop of Los Angeles since 2011. He previously served as Auxiliary Bishop of Denver from 2001–2004 and as Archbishop of San Antonio from 2004–2010.  
Beginning on November 15, 2016, Gomez served as Vice President of the United States Conference of Catholic Bishops; his term as Vice President ended with his election as President on November 12, 2019. He is the first person of Hispanic descent to hold both positions.
