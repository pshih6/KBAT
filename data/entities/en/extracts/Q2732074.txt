Gustavo Adolfo Madero González also known as "Ojo Parado" ("staring eye") since he had one glass eye. (16 January 1875 – 18 February 1913), born in Parras de la Fuente, Coahuila, Mexico, was a participant in the Mexican Revolution against Porfirio Díaz along with other members of his wealthy family.
Madero's brother, Francisco I. Madero, was president of Mexico from 1911 to 1913. During the coup d'état in Mexico City known as Ten Tragic Days, Gustavo Madero was arrested, released to followers of conspirator Félix Díaz, and killed by a mob. 
A borough in Mexico City is named after Gustavo A. Madero
