Todd Phillips (n√© Bunzl, born c. 1970/1971) is an American filmmaker and actor. He is known for writing and directing the films Road Trip (2000), Old School (2003), Starsky & Hutch (2004), The Hangover Trilogy (2009, 2011, and 2013),  and Due Date (2010). For his work on the satirical comedy film Borat (2006), Phillips was nominated for the Academy Award for Best Adapted Screenplay.
Phillips also expanded into other genres, directing the biographical crime film War Dogs (2016) and the psychological thriller film Joker (2019), the latter of which received the Golden Lion at the 76th Venice International Film Festival and earned him Academy Award nominations for Best Picture, Best Director, and Best Adapted Screenplay, alongside Scott Silver.
