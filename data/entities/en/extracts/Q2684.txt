Vladimir (Russian: Владимир, IPA: [vlɐdʲi'mʲɪr] (listen)) is a city and the administrative center of Vladimir Oblast, Russia, located on the Klyazma River, 200 kilometers (120 mi) to the east of Moscow. It is served by a railway and the M7 motorway. Its population is 345,373 (2010 Census); 315,954 (2002 Census); 349,702 (1989 Census). Vladimir has historical significance because it was Russia's capital in the 12th-13th centuries.
