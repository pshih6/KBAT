Sir Mokshagundam Visvesvaraya  FASc, more commonly known as Sir MV (15 September 1860 â€“ 14 April 1962) was an Indian civil engineer and statesman, and the 19th Diwan of Mysore, serving from 1912 to 1919. He received India's highest honour, Bharat Ratna, in 1955. He was knighted as a Knight Commander of the British Indian Empire (KCIE) by King George V for his contributions to public good. 15 September is celebrated as Engineer's Day in India, Sri Lanka and Tanzania in his memory. Visvesvaraya is held in high regard as a pre-eminent Engineer of India, and
was the Chief Engineer of Krishna Raja Sagara dam in North-West suburb of Mysuru city, and also served as the Chief Engineer of flood protection system for city of Hyderabad.
