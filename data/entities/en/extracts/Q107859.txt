Friedrich Märker  (March 7, 1893 in Augsburg, Bavaria – 27 April 1985, in Feldafing, Bavaria) was a German writer, essayist, theatre critic and publicist. His work focused on the physiognomy of the Nordic race (during the time of the Nazi regime), time and cultural criticism.
He also wrote under the pseudonyms Alexander Stark, Nicholas Haug and Fyodor Ukrainow.
