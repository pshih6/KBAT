JMJ Records was an American record label which was founded in 1989 by Jason Mizell, better known as Jam Master Jay from Run-D.M.C.. The label released such artists as The Afros, Fam-Lee, Onyx, Jayo Felony, Suga and 50 Cent. The label folded in 2002 after the murder of its founder. For 13 years of its existence, JMJ Records has released 6 studio albums and about 25 singles.
JMJ Records is currently run by Jam Master J'Son, the oldest son of the legendary Jam Master Jay.
