Erin Brockovich is a 2000 American biographical film directed by Steven Soderbergh and written by Susannah Grant. The film is a dramatization of the true story of Erin Brockovich, portrayed by Julia Roberts, who fought against the energy corporation Pacific Gas and Electric Company (PG&E). The film was a box office success, and critical reaction was positive.
Roberts won the Academy Award, BAFTA, Golden Globe Award, Screen Actors Guild Award, and various critics awards for Best Actress. The film itself was also nominated for the Best Picture. Steven Soderbergh, the film's director, was nominated for the Best Director for both this film and Traffic, but he won the award for Traffic.  Early in the film, the real Erin Brockovich has a cameo appearance as a waitress named Julia.
