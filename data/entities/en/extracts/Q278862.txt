Henry Porter (born 1953) is an English author and journalist. He is a writer of thrillers and was, until 2014, a regular columnist for The Observer. He is also the British editor of Vanity Fair and writes regularly on British and European politics for Vanity Fair's The Hive. He was on the Orwell Prize's journalism shortlist for 2009,[1] and wrote the award-winning novel Brandenburg, also titled Brandenburg Gate. He was on the shortlist for the Ian Fleming Steel Dagger for his novels Empire State and the Dying Light.
