Fabio Testi (born 2 August 1941) is an Italian film actor. After growing up witnessing film work done around Lake Garda, Testi entered the sets of the film and began work as a stuntman and a double on set, where he worked as a stuntman on The Good, the Bad, and the Ugly. Testi would continue stunt work and getting roles in low budge genre films until he was cast in Vittorio De Sica's film The Garden of the Finzi-Continis. Following this film, Testi became a star in Italy showing up in some artistic films by Giuseppe Patroni Griffi and Claude Chabrol. Testi would also continue work in genre films showing up in crime films in the 1970s as well as a few gialli. Testi's popularity as an actor slowed down towards the 1980s where he predominantly worked in television series. By the early 2000s, Testi also began showing in reality shows such as L'isola dei famosi.
