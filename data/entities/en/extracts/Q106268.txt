Stargard [ˈstarɡart] (listen) (German: Stargard in Pommern; Kashubian: Stôrgard; Latin: Stargardia) is a city in northwestern Poland, with a population of 71,017 (2005).  Situated on the Ina River it is the capital of Stargard County and since 1999 has been in the West Pomeranian Voivodeship; prior to that it was in the Szczecin Voivodeship (1975–1998).
The city's name is of Pomeranian (Kashubian) origin and stands for old (stari) town/city (gard or gôrd). It's one of the biggest towns of Szczecin agglomeration. Stargard is a major railroad junction, where the southwards connection from Szczecin splits into two directions - one towards Poznań and the other towards Gdańsk. There is also another minor line to Pyrzyce from the town.
From 1950 until December 31, 2015, the town was known as Stargard Szczeciński.
