Foxes is a 1980 American coming of age drama film directed by Adrian Lyne, in his feature directorial debut, and written by Gerald Ayres. The film stars Jodie Foster, Scott Baio, Sally Kellerman, Randy Quaid, and Cherie Currie, in her acting debut. It revolves around a group of teenage girls coming of age in suburban Los Angeles toward the end of the disco era.
Foxes was theatrically released on February 29, 1980, by PolyGram Pictures.
The film was Foster's penultimate major film appearance before taking a sabbatical from acting to attend Yale. It received several positive reviews from critics and was a moderate box office success grossing $7,470,348 in North America. The film has attained a cult status and is often cited amongst the greatest teenage-centric films.
