In 1787 he became professor of philosophy at Leipzig. Writing works on Spinoza in the late 1780s, he became increasingly influenced by Immanuel Kant: his Betrachtungen (1790-1)  was "the first real example of a Kantian philosophical theology". Forced to give up his professorship in 1797, he died unsalaried.
