The National Distance Education University, known in Spanish as Universidad Nacional de Educaci√≥n a Distancia (UNED), is a distance learning and research university founded in 1972 and is the only university run by the central government of Spain. It has headquarters in Madrid, Spain, with campuses in all Spanish autonomous communities. In addition, there are 14 study centres, and 3 exam points, in 13 countries in Europe, Americas and Africa. The University awards undergraduate and postgraduate degrees, as well as non-degree qualifications such as diplomas and certificates, or continuing education units.
UNED combines traditional onsite education with distance learning programs. With over 150.000 students, UNED is the largest university in Spain and the second largest in Europe.
