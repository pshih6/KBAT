Nikolay Robertovich Erdman (Russian: Николай Робертович Эрдман, IPA: [nʲɪkɐˈlaj ˈrobʲɪrtəvʲɪtɕ ˈɛrdmən] (listen); 16 November [O.S. 3 November] 1900, Moscow — 10 August 1970) was a Soviet dramatist and screenwriter primarily remembered for his work with  Vsevolod Meyerhold in the 1920s. His plays, notably The Suicide (1928), form a link in Russian literary history between the satirical drama of Nikolai Gogol and the post-World War II Theatre of the Absurd.
