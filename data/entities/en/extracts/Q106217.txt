Samuel (Sammy) Gronemann was the son of the chief rabbi of Hannover. Gronemann became one of chief contributors to the satirical magazine Schlemiel (which ran 1903-1905, 1919-1924). He fled to Paris in 1933 after the rise of the Nazis, and immigrated to Mandate Palestine in 1936.
