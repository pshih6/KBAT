Haji Abdul Qadeer (Pashto: حاجی عبدالقدیر‎; c. 1951 – July 6, 2002)  was a Pashtun leader in Afghanistan. Qadeer was a prominent member of the Northern Alliance and opposed the Taliban. He served as the head of Eastern Afghanistan Shura and later Vice President of Afghanistan and Minister of Public Works in the administration of Hamid Karzai from 19 June 2002 until his assassination on 6 July 2002.
Qadeer belonged to the influential Pashtun Arsala family from Nangarhar province of Afghanistan. His brother was the anti-Soviet and Northern Alliance leader  Abdul Haq, who was executed in late 2001 by the Taliban. From 1992 to 1996, before the Taliban gained power, Abdul Qadeer was the governor of Nangahar province.
