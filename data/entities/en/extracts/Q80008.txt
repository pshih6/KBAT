Hope is a city in Hempstead County in southwestern Arkansas, United States. Hope is the county seat of Hempstead County and the principal city of the Hope Micropolitan Statistical Area, which includes all of Hempstead and Nevada counties. As of the 2010 census the population was 10,095, and in 2018 the population was estimated at 9,715.Hope is the birthplace of two long-serving Arkansas governors: Bill Clinton (who went on to become the 42nd president of the United States after the 1992 election) and Mike Huckabee (who ran for the Republican presidential nomination in 2008 and 2016). It is also the birthplace of former White House Press Secretary Sarah Huckabee Sanders.
