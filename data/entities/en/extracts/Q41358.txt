Styria (German: Steiermark [ˈʃtaɪ̯ɐˌmaʁk] (listen); Croatian and Slovene: Štajerska; Hungarian: Stájerország; Czech: Štýrsko; Slovak: Štajersko) is a state, or Bundesland, located in the southeast of Austria. Covering 16,401 km2 (6,332 sq mi), Styria is the second largest of the nine component states of Austria (also sometimes referred to as provinces in English), after Lower Austria. Styria's neighbor to the South is Slovenia (Carinthia Statistical Region, Drava Statistical Region and Mura Statistical Region). Within Austria, the contiguous states are Carinthia, Salzburg, Upper Austria, Lower Austria, and The Burgenland.  The state capital is Graz, which had 276,526 inhabitants at the beginning of 2015.
