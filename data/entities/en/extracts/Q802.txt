Nowy Sącz (Polish: pronounced [ˈnɔvɘ̟ˈsɔ̃ʈ͡ʂ]; Hungarian: Újszandec; Silesian: Nowy Sůncz) is a city in the Lesser Poland Voivodeship of southern Poland. It is the district capital of Nowy Sącz County as a separate administrative unit. Founded by the Duke of Cracow on 8 November 1292, New Sacz (known also by other names) is one of the oldest cities in the Lesser Poland region, with a population of around 83,896 as of 2018.
