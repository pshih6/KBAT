Reiko Mori (森礼子, April 29, 1922 - December 27, 2012), known by her stage name Noriko Sengoku (千石規子, Sengoku Noriko), was a Japanese film and television actress active primarily in the 1950s and 1960s. She made her film debut in 1947 and starred in several of Akira Kurosawa's early films such as Drunken Angel (1948), The Quiet Duel (1949), Stray Dog (1949), Scandal (1950), The Idiot (1951) and Seven Samurai (1954).
