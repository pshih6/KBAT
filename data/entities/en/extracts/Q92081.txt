Heinrich August Luyken (10 December 1864 in Altenkirchen – 21 September 1947 in Amersham) is an author of various adventure novels in Esperanto.
Heinrich Luyken attended gymnasium in Düsseldorf. In 1885 he emigrated to England and acquired citizenship. He married to the Englishwoman Alice Maud Marian Newbon and they had four children.
He learnt Esperanto in the year 1904 and he soon began propagating it. Heinrich Luyken is considered one of the main writers of the Esperanto literature before the Second World War. He published four adventure novels between 1912 and 1924, in which his evangelical faith is reflected. In every roman the protagonist is a heretic who is "saved" by their faith.
