Prince Vladimir Fyodorovich Odoyevsky (Russian: Владимир Фёдорович Одоевский, IPA: [ɐˈdojɪfskʲɪj]; 11 August [O.S. 30 July] 1803 – 11 March [O.S. 27 February] 1869) was a prominent Russian philosopher, writer, music critic, philanthropist and pedagogue. He became known as the "Russian Hoffmann" on account of his keen interest in phantasmagoric tales and musical criticism.
