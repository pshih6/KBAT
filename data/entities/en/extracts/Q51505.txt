Jean-Baptiste Antoine Marcelin Marbot, known as Marcellin Marbot (Pronunciation:  mahr-BOH, French: [ʒɑ̃ batist ɑ̃twan maʁsølɛ̃ maʁbo]; August 18, 1782 – November 16, 1854), was a French general, famous for his memoirs depicting the Napoleonic age of warfare. He belongs to a family that has distinguished itself particularly in the career of arms, giving three generals to France in less than 50 years. His elder brother, Antoine Adolphe Marcelin Marbot, was also a military man of some note.
