Robert Montgomery (; born Henry Montgomery Jr.; May 21, 1904 â€“ September 27, 1981) was an American film and television actor, director, and producer. He was also the father of actress Elizabeth Montgomery. He began his acting career on the stage, but was soon hired by MGM.  Initially assigned roles in comedies, he soon proved he was able to handle dramatic ones as well.  During World War II, he drove ambulances in France until the Dunkirk evacuation.  When the United States entered the war on December 8, 1941, he enlisted in the Navy, and was present at the invasion at Normandy.  After the war, he returned to Hollywood, where he worked in both films and, later, in television.
