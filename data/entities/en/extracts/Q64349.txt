August Thyssen  (German: [ˈaʊɡʊst ˈtʏsən]; Eschweiler, 17 May 1842 – Landsberg Castle, Ratingen, near Kettwig, 4 April 1926) was a successful German industrialist who funded Pan-German and Nazi government war conquest. As most Pan-Germans, he remained out of the public eye and his affiliations with Pan-Germans and Nazis remain largely unknown.
