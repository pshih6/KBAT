The Varsinais-Suomi constituency (Finnish: Varsinais-Suomen vaalipiiri, Swedish: Egentliga Finlands valkrets) is a Finnish constituency. It covers the administrative region of Southwest Finland, with a population of 450,968 (as of 2002). Varsinais-Suomi elects 17 members to the Parliament of Finland.
The constituency's profile is urban, centred on the cities of Turku and Salo. The largest party in elections since 1999 has been the National Coalition Party.
