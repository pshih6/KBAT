Marie Huber (4 March 1695 – 13 June 1753) was a Genevan writer on theology and related subjects, as well as a translator and editor, at a time when it was rare for a female writer to write about theology.Huber was a proponent of universalism, and was considered by some a deist.  Her Letters Concerning the Religion Essential to Man (1761) are known to have been read, in translation, by Robert Burns.She was one of 15 children, and was the great-aunt of François Huber, the naturalist.
