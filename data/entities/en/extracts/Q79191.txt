Jelena Karleuša Tošić (Serbian Cyrillic: Јелена Карлеуша; born 17 August 1978) is a Serbian singer and media personality. She rose to prominence in the mid-1990s as a turbo-folk singer, upon her debut album Ogledalce, but as her career progressed she tried escape that image. During the following decades she saw commercial success with her pop crossover attempts JK Revolution (2008) and Diva (2012). These releases were followed by two Belgrade concerts: All About Diva (2010) and Viva la Diva Show (2013); before 15,000 and 40,000 people, respectively. She has also served as a judge and mentor on the televised singing contest Zvezde Granda since 2015. German magazine Focus referred to her as the "Madonna of the Balkans", while W magazine compared her to Lady Gaga. Having sold over million records, while also being one of the highest payed regional performers, Karleuša is regarded as one of the most popular Serbian musicians.In addition, Karleuša has developed a strong social media presence through her fashion style, while gaining international media recognition by drawing comparisons to Kim Kardashian and Beyoncé in 2015. The following year, she was the first Serbian woman, and second person overall after Novak Djokovic, to reach one million followers on Instagram.Since 2008, Karleuša has been married to footballer Duško Tošić, with whom she has two daughters. Outside of her music career, she is noted for her comments on politics, specially regarding LGBT rights and social equality. Her private life alongside her outspoken views, have also received significant media coverage and have sparked occasional controversy.
