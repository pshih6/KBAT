Emile Berliner (May 20, 1851 â€“ August 3, 1929), originally Emil Berliner, was a German-born inventor. He is best known for inventing the vertical-cut flat disc record (called a "gramophone record" in British and American English) used with a Phonograph. He founded the United States Gramophone Company in 1894, The Gramophone Company in London, England, in 1897, Deutsche Grammophon in Hanover, Germany, in 1898, Berliner Gram-o-phone Company of Canada in Montreal in 1899 (chartered in 1904), and Victor Talking Machine Company in 1901 with Eldridge Johnson.
