Cub Records was an American record label, and a subsidiary of MGM Records, which started in 1958 for rhythm and blues releases.Artists who released records on Cub included The Impalas ("Sorry (I Ran All the Way Home)"), Jimmy Jones ("Handy Man", "Good Timin'"), the Stereos ("I Really Love You"), and Jimmy Velvit ("Sometimes At Night", "We Belong Together").  The label lasted until 1968.
