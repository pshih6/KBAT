Liang Qichao (Chinese: 梁啓超; Cantonese: Lèuhng Kái-chīu; 23 February 1873 – 19 January 1929), also known as Liang Ch'i-Ch'ao; courtesy name Zhuoru, art name Rengong, was a Chinese historian, journalist, philosopher, and politician who lived during the late Qing dynasty and the early Republic of China. He inspired Chinese scholars with his writings and reform movements.
