Time of the Innocent (German: Die Zeit der Schuldlosen) is a 1964 West German drama film directed by Thomas Fantl, based on a play by Siegfried Lenz. It was distributed by Columbia-Bavaria Filmgesellschaft in Germany and represented by Export-Film Bischoff worldwide. It was entered into the 14th Berlin International Film Festival. It opened in the presence of President Lübke and his wife, German Chancellor Erhard, Vice Chancellor Mende, Secretary of State Schröder, Minister Westrick, "Bundespressechef" von Haase in Berlin's famous Zoo-Palast, which is one of the highest attendances of German politician to a Berlinale premiere.
