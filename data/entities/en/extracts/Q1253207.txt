R. Stanton Avery (January 13, 1907 â€“ December 12, 1997) was an American inventor, most known for creating self-adhesive labels (modern stickers). Using a $100 loan, and combining used machine parts with a saber saw, he created and patented the world's first self-adhesive (also called pressure sensitive) die-cut labeling machine. In 1935, he founded what is now the Avery Dennison Corporation.Avery served as chairman of the board of trustees of California Institute of Technology, and was a member of the board of trustees of the Huntington Library and the board of trustees of the Los Angeles County Museum of Art.Avery House at Caltech is named for him.
