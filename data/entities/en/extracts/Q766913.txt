Edward Rudolph "Ed" Bradley, Jr. (June 22, 1941 â€“ November 9, 2006) was an American journalist, best known for 26 years of award-winning work on the CBS News television program 60 Minutes.  During his earlier career he also covered the fall of Saigon, was the first black television correspondent to cover the White House, and anchored his own news broadcast, CBS Sunday Night News with Ed Bradley. He received several awards for his work including the Peabody, the National Association of Black Journalists Lifetime Achievement Award, Radio Television Digital News Association Paul White (journalist) Award and 19 Emmy Awards.
